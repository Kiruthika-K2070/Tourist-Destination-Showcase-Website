Gyeongbokgung Palace - Seoul

Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, served as the main royal palace of the Joseon Dynasty. It showcases traditional Korean architecture and cultural history. Visitors can explore vast courtyards, pavilions, the National Folk Museum, and witness the royal guard changing ceremony. Nestled in the heart of Seoul, this landmark blends historical richness with scenic beauty, making it a must-visit for history lovers and photographers alike.

N Seoul Tower (Namsan Tower) - Seoul

Standing atop Namsan Mountain, N Seoul Tower offers panoramic views of Seoul's skyline. Originally a communication and observation tower, it's now a popular romantic spot known for its "Locks of Love" fence. Visitors can enjoy cafes, restaurants, an observatory, and walking trails leading up to the tower. At night, the tower glows beautifully, making it one of the city's most iconic attractions.

Bukchon Hanok Village - Seoul

Bukchon Hanok Village is a traditional Korean neighborhood nestled between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung Palaces. It features hundreds of preserved hanok (traditional Korean houses), offering a glimpse into Seoul's past. Walking through its narrow lanes, visitors experience a peaceful contrast to the modern cityscape, surrounded by cultural centers, artisan shops, and stunning rooftop views. It's a perfect destination for cultural exploration and scenic photography.

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) - Seoul

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) is a major urban landmark known for its futuristic architecture by Zaha Hadid. It serves as a design and cultural hub, hosting exhibitions, fashion shows, product launches, and events. The LED rose garden and seamless curved structure make it a favorite for both tourists and photographers. DDP also connects directly to a bustling shopping district, blending modern design with city life.

Jeju Island - Jeju Province

Jeju Island is South Korea's largest island and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its volcanic landscapes, Hallasan Mountain, lava tubes, waterfalls, and coastal beauty, it's a paradise for nature lovers. Popular among honeymooners and outdoor adventurers, Jeju offers scenic hiking trails, peaceful beaches, local cuisine, and unique cultural experiences unlike anywhere else in Korea.

Seoraksan National Park - Gangwon Province

Seoraksan National Park is a natural wonderland known for its dramatic rocky peaks, deep valleys, and lush forests. It's especially popular in autumn when the foliage turns vibrant red and gold. Visitors can hike scenic trails, ride a cable car, or visit the ancient Sinheungsa Temple nestled within the mountains. It's an unforgettable spot for those seeking adventure and breathtaking views.

Hwaseong Fortress - Suwon

Hwaseong Fortress is an 18th-century walled structure built by King Jeongjo of the Joseon dynasty. Spanning over 5.7 kilometers, it features beautiful gates, observation towers, and artillery facilities. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it blends traditional architecture with military innovation. Visitors can walk the fortress path, enjoy panoramic views of Suwon, and take part in cultural performances and archery experiences.

Andong Hahoe Folk Village - Andong

Andong Hahoe Folk Village is a well-preserved traditional village that showcases Korea's Confucian culture and architecture from the Joseon era. Surrounded by mountains and rivers, the village features tiled-roof hanoks, traditional rituals, and the famous Mask Dance Festival. It offers an authentic glimpse into Korea's past, making it a culturally rich and peaceful destination for visitors.

Nami Island (Chuncheon)

Nami Island is a half-moon-shaped island located near Chuncheon, famous for its picturesque tree-lined roads. Popularized by Korean dramas, the island offers stunning seasonal scenery, bike rentals, art installations, and peaceful nature walks. With its romantic atmosphere and eco-friendly charm, it's an ideal getaway from the city.

View on Google Maps

Haeundae Beach - Busan

Haeundae Beach is Busan's most famous seaside destination, known for its soft sand, summer festivals, and scenic coastal skyline. The beach is surrounded by skyscrapers, luxury hotels, and lively markets. Visitors enjoy sunbathing, seafood, water sports, and evening strolls along the shore. It's the perfect blend of urban energy and natural beauty.
